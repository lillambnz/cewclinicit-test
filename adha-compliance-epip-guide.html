8 Pillars of ADHA Compliance

Essential requirements for healthcare practices to remain compliant and eligible for ePIP incentives.

Healthcare Provider Identifiers

Register for HPI-O and HPI-I. Enable Individual Healthcare Identifiers lookup for patients.

My Health Record Integration

Connect your practice management system to My Health Record and upload clinical documents securely.

Secure Messaging

Implement RACGP-approved secure messaging and configure encrypted referrals and results delivery.

Electronic Prescribing

Enable electronic prescribing and integrate with PBS and Real-Time Prescription Monitoring.

Multi-Factor Authentication

Implement MFA for all users accessing patient data to meet ADHA authentication requirements.

Data Loss Prevention

Configure DLP policies to prevent unauthorized sharing of patient data and monitor access.

Patient Privacy & Data Security

Encryption at rest and in transit with role-based access controls for clinical data.

Annual Compliance Audits

Maintain audit documentation with regular security assessments and penetration testing.

ADHA Compliance Roadmap

A practical sequence to achieve full compliance and maintain it long term.

  • Step 1: Healthcare Provider Registration Register for HPI-O and HPI-I and enable My Health Record access.
  • Step 2: Secure Communications Setup Deploy RACGP-approved secure messaging and encrypted email.
  • Step 3: Security Hardening MFA, DLP policies, and endpoint encryption across all devices.
  • Step 4: ePIP Compliance Configure practice systems, enable eHealth features, and prepare documentation.
  • Step 5: Ongoing Compliance Annual audits, staff training, and continuous policy updates.

ADHA Compliance FAQs

Answers to the most common compliance questions from Australian healthcare practices.

What is the difference between HPI-O and HPI-I?

HPI-O is for organisations while HPI-I is for individual practitioners. Both are required for My Health Record access.

How long does My Health Record integration take?

Typically 2 to 4 weeks depending on your practice management system and existing infrastructure.

What happens if we do not achieve ADHA compliance?

Non-compliance can result in loss of ePIP payments, restricted My Health Record access, and regulatory action.

Can ePIP funding cover IT infrastructure costs?

Yes. ePIP payments can help offset compliant infrastructure costs. We help you maximize eligible claims.

What is the cost of ADHA compliance implementation?

Costs vary by clinic size and current systems. We provide a free assessment and tailored quote.
